Working Name & Tagline
Primary Name: AlphaArena
Tagline: Practice, Backtest, and Prove Your Edge.
Who It’s For (Role-Based Tracks)

Alpha Researcher / Quant – feature engineering, factor modeling, statistical tests, ML for time series.
Quant Developer / Data Engineer – low-latency, robust pipelines, kdb+/q, Python/C++ infra.
Portfolio Manager / Risk – portfolio optimization, scenario analysis, VaR/ES, limits & constraints.
Execution / Microstructure – order book modeling, impact costs, routing, slippage, best execution.
Ops / Compliance / Model Risk – reproducibility, audit trails, SR 11‑7 style documentation, data governance.
Core Idea (How It’s Like LeetCode, But for Funds)
Like LeetCode, AlphaArena is a structured, tiered problem set with an in-browser coding environment.
Unlike LeetCode, problems are scored by investment performance & risk (Sharpe, drawdown, turnover, stability), not only algorithmic correctness. Users run backtests in-sandbox against synthetic and anonymized “Data Rooms” designed to mimic real market conditions and constraints, with hidden out-of-sample datasets to prevent overfitting.
Key Features
Problem Library & Difficulty Tiers
Tiers: Foundation (Easy), Practitioner (Medium), Expert (Hard), plus “Market Day” (live-sim) scenarios.
Domains/Tracks:
Alpha & Factors: Fama-French style factors, alternative data signals, stationarity & regime detection.
Portfolio: Markowitz & convex optimization with constraints (leverage, turnover, liquidity), robust optimization.
Risk: VaR/ES, stress scenarios, scenario design, drawdown control, model validation.
Execution & Microstructure: VWAP/TWAP, Almgren–Chriss, limit order book sim, impact models.
Derivatives: Options greeks, implied vol surface, delta-hedging PnL attribution.
Data Engineering: ETL on large tick data, schema design, kdb+/q queries, time-zone/calendar handling.
ML for Markets: Walk-forward CV, leakage control, nested CV, online learning, feature drift checks.
Compliance & Model Risk: Auditability, documentation, reproducibility, data lineage.
In-Browser Coding & Notebooks
Languages: Python, C++, q/kdb+, R, Julia, SQL.
Editors: Code editor + research notebook mode (Jupyter-like) for narrative, charts, and results.
Sandboxed Backtester: Deterministic, parameterized execution simulator (slippage, fees, latency, borrow) with hidden out-of-sample validation.
Data Rooms: Curated synthetic datasets (daily, intraday, options, news sentiment, fundamentals) with realistic artifacts (delistings, survivorship bias, corporate actions).
Scoring Beyond Correctness
Primary Score: Composite of Sharpe, Sortino, Max Drawdown, Turnover, Capacity, and Stability.
Robustness Checks: Walk-forward tests, regime shifts, bootstrap resampling, leakage detection.
Performance vs. Baselines: Compare against buy-and-hold, classic factor sleeves, or benchmark optimizers.
Learning Paths & Interview Prep
Role-based tracks: “Alpha Researcher,” “Quant Dev,” “Risk,” “Execution,” “Derivatives.”
Interview simulators: Timed coding rounds + case studies (e.g., “Design a robust backtest for alt‑data earnings momentum”).
Solution Walkthroughs: Editorial and community solutions with research write-ups (assumptions, pitfalls, sensitivity).
Contests & Tournaments (“Alpha Tournaments”)
Periodic competitions on unseen datasets; score by out-of-sample performance & robustness.
Leaderboards: “Sharpe Leaderboard,” “Risk-Adjusted PnL,” “Execution Efficiency.”
Team mode: PM + Quant + Dev teams collaborate in private rooms.
Enterprise & Hiring Modules
Employer Dashboards: Custom problem sets; calibrate difficulty; score candidates on risk-adjusted performance and code quality.
SSO, RBAC, Private Data Rooms: Firms can upload anonymized or synthetic internal datasets into private challenges.
Compliance & Audit: Full reproducibility, environment capture, artifact logging, model cards.
Community & Resources
Discussion Forum & Research Notes: Peer reviews of strategy write-ups; checklist for data pitfalls.
Curricula: From market foundations to advanced microstructure and ML for trading; guided paths.
Mentor Office Hours: Weekly live sessions; recordings; curated reading lists.
Example Challenges (Sample Prompts)
Foundation (Easy)
Compute rolling beta and idiosyncratic volatility relative to a benchmark; test stability across regimes.
Clean and align corporate actions; ensure your total-return series matches reference checks.
Practitioner (Medium)
Build a cross-sectional alpha combining value, quality, and sentiment. Enforce turnover < 20% monthly.
Implement an execution strategy (VWAP/TWAP hybrid) with transient impact; report slippage vs. arrival price.
Expert (Hard)
Construct an implied vol surface with arbitrage constraints; price path-dependent options and compute greeks.
Design a walk-forward ML pipeline for intraday signals (feature drift detection, nested CV, leakage guards).
Robust portfolio optimization with cardinality and sector constraints under liquidity caps; stress with 2008/2020-like scenarios.
Compliance / Model Risk
Produce a model card: assumptions, data lineage, validation, limitations, policy flags, and monitoring plan.
Detect and document potential data leakage in a proposed alt-data pipeline (e.g., news timestamps vs. exchange events).

Differentiators vs. LeetCode
Performance-Based Scoring: Emphasis on risk-adjusted PnL and robustness, not just algorithm outputs.
Market Sim & Data Rooms: Realistic slippage, fees, borrow, corporate actions, and out-of-sample validation.
Research Literacy: Requires write-ups and sensitivity analysis; combats overfitting and data snooping.
Enterprise Compliance: Reproducibility, audit logs, RBAC, model cards—aligned with real fund governance.

Interview Mode: Timed prompts, automated report + code quality checks (lint, complexity, tests).
Enterprise Pilot: SSO, private challenges, role-based dashboards, exportable reports.
Metrics of Success
Candidate throughput and enterprise adoption (time-to-hire reduction, better candidate signal).

Community health: solution write-ups, peer reviews, moderated discussion quality.
Contest integrity: low leakage rates; consistency between in-sample and out-of-sample results.
